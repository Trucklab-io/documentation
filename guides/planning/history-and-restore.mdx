title: "Use planning history and restore"
description: "Search planning versions, inspect their entries, open a snapshot, and restore approved work."

Planning history is the audit view for Draft, Submitted, Approved, and Rejected
versions. Use it to verify what happened before changing current planning.

## Find a version

Open **Planning history** and use:

- search by date, route, client, or change summary;
- the **Client** filter;
- the **Status** filter;
- 15, 25, or 50 results per page;
- **Refresh** to reload the history.

Select **Clear filters** when the expected version is not visible.

## Read a history row

Each version shows the planning day, client, version number and status, trip count,
route previews, and author or change context. Expand or open the version when you
need its complete entries rather than relying on the preview.

## Open a historical version

Select the row action to open that version in Planning. Affretio changes the Planning
view to the chosen snapshot. Reconfirm the version badge before using any action.

## Restore an approved version

Use restore when an earlier accepted snapshot should become the basis for new work.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the approved snapshot">
    Filter history to **Approved** and locate the correct client and day.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open and compare">
    Open the version and compare its complete trip list with the current operation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Restore">
    Open **Actions** and select **Restaurer**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the new version">
    Affretio creates another version based on the snapshot. Check current drivers,
    trucks, absences, routes, and prices.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit and approve">
    Move the restored content through the normal review workflow.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Restore creates a new version. It does not overwrite, delete, or renumber the history.
</Note>

<Warning>
  An earlier approved plan can contain assignments or commercial data that are no
  longer valid. Restore the structure, then validate it against today's resources.
</Warning>
