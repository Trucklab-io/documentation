Clients are the organizations your transport company plans trips for. Client data
also scopes recurring trips, tariff grids, planning history, tracking, and billing work.

## Create a client

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Clients">
    Select **Clients** in the sidebar, or open **Home** and select **Open details**
    for Clients.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start a new client">
    Select **New client**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Basics">
    Enter **Client name**. Use the operational name dispatchers expect to see in
    planning selectors and exports.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add the Location">
    Enter at least three address characters to display suggestions. Select the
    correct suggestion to populate the address, city, country, and postal code,
    or enter these fields manually.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and create">
    Check **Basics** and **Location**, then select **Create client**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Only the client name is required, but a complete location makes route creation
and data review faster.

## Find a client

Use **Search clients** to match the client name, country, city, postal code, and
related recurring-trip or tariff counts. Choose 15, 25, or 50 records per page.
Select **Clear filters** when a known client does not appear.

## Read the directory

Each client row helps you review:

- the organization name;
- location information;
- associated recurring trips;
- associated tariff grids;
- edit actions.

These counts are useful before changing or deleting a record.

## Edit a client

1. Find the client.
2. Select the row's edit action.
3. Update **Basics** or **Location**.
4. Review the complete record.
5. Select **Save client**.

Changing the client name updates how the same record appears across the workspace.
Its existing planning and operational associations remain connected.

## Before deleting or replacing a client

Check the client's:

- current and historical planning versions;
- recurring trip templates;
- tariff grids;
- tracking shares, charters, and pre-invoicing work.

<Warning>
  Do not delete and recreate a client to correct its name or address. Edit the
  existing record so planning and operational history remain connected.
</Warning>

## Data-quality checklist

- Use one client record per legal or operational entity.
- Avoid abbreviations that different dispatchers may interpret differently.
- Confirm the suggested address before accepting it.
- Search before creating a record to prevent duplicates.
- Keep tariff grids scoped to the correct client after mergers or name changes.
