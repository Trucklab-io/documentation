Truck records represent the fleet vehicles dispatchers assign to trips. Each
record has an operating organization, registration, type, optional driver, status,
purchase date, and odometer value.

## Before you begin

An Owner must complete **Settings → Company → Own company profile**. Affretio
disables **New truck** until the operating company exists.

## Create a truck

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Trucks">
    Select **Trucks** in the sidebar or use **Open details** on Home.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Basics">
    Select **New truck**, choose the operating company, enter the registration,
    and choose or enter the truck type.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Assignment">
    Choose an eligible driver or leave the truck unassigned. Set **Available**,
    **On route**, or **Maintenance**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Technical information">
    Enter the purchase date and odometer value when known.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and confirm">
    Review every section, select the confirmation, and create the truck.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Affretio suggests common types such as Semi-trailer, Rigid truck, Tanker, Box truck,
Flatbed, and Refrigerated. Use a consistent vocabulary across drivers, trucks, and tariff grids.

## Truck statuses

| Status | Meaning | Planning guidance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Available** | Ready for operational assignment. | Assign normally. |
| **On route** | Currently operating. | Check timing before another assignment. |
| **Maintenance** | Temporarily stopped. | Do not assign until it returns to service. |

## Search and filters

Search the fleet by registration, truck type, driver, or organization. Filter by
status and truck type. Choose 15, 25, or 50 records per page when maintaining a larger fleet.

## Change a regular driver

1. Find the truck and select edit.
2. Open **Assignment**.
3. Choose another eligible driver or leave it unassigned.
4. Review and save the truck.
5. Review current planning separately.

Changing the truck record does not automatically rewrite historical assignments.

## Perform bulk updates

Select the intended rows and use the bulk toolbar to apply a status or delete the
selected records. Confirm the selection count and clear filters if you need to see
the complete fleet before acting.

<Warning>
  A bulk Maintenance or delete action can affect many planning choices. Review the
  selected registrations and active planning before confirming it.
</Warning>

## Keep fleet data reliable

- Normalize registration spelling and punctuation.
- Update status as vehicles enter or leave maintenance.
- Record odometer readings using one team-wide cadence.
- Use truck types that match tariff-grid scopes.
- Edit a correction instead of creating a duplicate.
- Check active and historical planning before deletion.
