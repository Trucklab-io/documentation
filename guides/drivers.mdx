Driver records make people available for truck and trip assignment. They hold
identity, contact, preferred truck type, availability, and mobile-access controls.

## Create a driver

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Drivers">
    Select **Drivers** in the sidebar or use **Open details** on Home.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start a driver record">
    Select **New driver**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Identity">
    Choose the operating organization and enter the driver's first and last name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Contact information">
    Enter the phone number and address when available. Choose the preferred truck type.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and create">
    Verify the identity, organization, and contact summary, then create the driver.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Find and filter drivers

Search by driver name, phone, truck type, assigned registration, or organization.
Use directory filters to narrow availability and truck type. Clear filters before
assuming a driver record is missing.

The availability badge can show:

- **Disponible** when no current absence covers today;
- **Absent · Vacances** for vacation;
- **Absent · Maladie** for sick leave;
- **Absent · Autre absence** for another operational absence.

## Record an absence

1. Find the driver and open the availability control.
2. Choose **Vacances**, **Maladie**, or **Autre absence**.
3. Enter the required **Du** and **Au** dates.
4. Add an optional note containing only operationally necessary information.
5. Save the absence.

The panel shows the current status and future absences. You can edit or delete an
incorrect period. Use **Marquer comme disponible** to end the current unavailability
when the driver returns earlier than expected.

<Warning>
  Do not place medical details or other sensitive personal information in absence
  notes. Dispatchers need the availability period, not a diagnosis.
</Warning>

## How absences affect planning

Affretio prevents a conflicting driver assignment when the trip date overlaps a
recorded absence. You can choose another driver or use **Assigner plus tard**.
Correct the absence only when its dates are wrong.

## Create driver mobile access

Open the driver's actions and choose the mobile-access option. Affretio displays a
driver-specific link.

- Treat the link as access information.
- Copy it only for the intended driver.
- Use a secure channel.
- Revoke driver mobile sessions when a device or link is no longer trusted.

## Edit, delete, and bulk update

Edit a record when contact or preferred truck information changes. The bulk toolbar
can update selected drivers or delete several selected records, depending on your role.

Before deletion, check:

- assigned trucks;
- current and historical planning;
- recurring trip defaults;
- mobile sessions and operational history.

<Note>
  Use an absence to hide a driver temporarily from assignments. Deletion is for a
  record that should no longer remain in the workspace and is not protected by references.
</Note>
