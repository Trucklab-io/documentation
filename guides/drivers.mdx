Driver records make people available for assignment in planning. They also hold
contact information, preferred truck type, availability periods, and mobile access.

## Create a driver

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Drivers">
    Open **Home**, find Drivers, and select **Open details**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start a driver record">
    Select **New driver**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter identity information">
    In **Identity**, select the company and enter the driver's first and last name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add contact and operating details">
    In **Contact**, add the phone number, address, and preferred truck type when available.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and create">
    Review the identity and contact summary, confirm the information, and create the driver.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Record an absence

Use a driver availability panel to prevent assignments during an absence.

1. Open **Drivers** and locate the driver.
2. Open the driver's availability or absence controls.
3. Choose the reason and enter the **Du** and **Au** dates.
4. Add optional context without including sensitive medical details.
5. Save the absence.

The Planning screen marks an unavailable driver and prevents conflicting assignments
for covered dates.

<Warning>
  Record only the operational information dispatchers need. Do not add medical or
  other sensitive personal data to the optional note.
</Warning>

## Create a driver mobile link

Open the driver's actions and choose the mobile access option. Affretio displays a
driver-specific link that you can share through a secure channel.

- Treat the link as access information.
- Send it only to the intended driver.
- Revoke driver mobile sessions if a device or link is no longer trusted.

## Update or remove a driver

Edit the existing record when a driver's contact details or truck type changes.
Before deleting a driver, check active planning, recurring trips, truck assignment,
and planning history.

<Note>
  If a driver should temporarily disappear from assignments, record an absence
  instead of deleting the driver.
</Note>
