Affretio organizes your transport operation into three connected layers: your
workspace, operational resources, and planning.

## Main areas

| Area | What you manage |
| --- | --- |
| **Home** | A live summary of driver availability, fleet status, and client activity. |
| **Dashboard** | Fleet utilization, planning status, recent activity, and setup health. |
| **Clients** | The organizations you plan and price trips for. |
| **Drivers** | Driver identity, contact details, truck type, availability, and mobile access. |
| **Trucks** | Registration, type, assigned driver, status, purchase date, and odometer. |
| **Planning** | Daily trips, driver and truck assignments, versions, printing, and PDF exports. |
| **Operations** | Owner/Admin-only pre-invoicing, alerts, charters, carriers, tracking links, routes, and ETA. |
| **Planning history** | Previous planning versions and their review status. |
| **Recurring trips** | Daily, weekly, or monthly trip templates. |
| **Settings** | Company profile, workspace users, roles, status, and tariff grids. |

## Recommended setup order

<Steps>
  <Step title="Complete your company profile">
    Open **Settings → Company**. Your own company is the operating organization
    for drivers, trucks, and workspace settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a client">
    Planning is created for a client organization, so you need at least one client before planning trips.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add drivers and trucks">
    Create the resources you want to assign. A truck can remain unassigned until you know its regular driver.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create and review a planning">
    Build trips in a draft, submit the version, and have an Owner or Admin approve it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Affretio always works inside the selected workspace. If your user can access
  more than one workspace, verify the active workspace before making changes.
</Info>

## Resource relationships

- Your company profile represents the transport company operating the workspace.
- Drivers and trucks belong to that operation.
- A driver may have an assigned truck and periods of unavailability.
- A trip belongs to a client planning and can be assigned to a driver and truck.
- Recurring trips create occurrences when you open the relevant planning date.
- Approved planning versions become the active operational schedule.
- Owners and Admins can follow approved transport work in **Operations**.
